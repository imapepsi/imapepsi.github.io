A Little About Myself

My life is all about learning new things; I’ll dive into any area that catches my interest. I love legos, stop motion animation, drawing, yo-yo tricks, budgeting, movies, art museums, books, developing mobile apps, and more. When I began programming in my senior year of high school, I figured out how to replicate my favorite math magic trick in Python. That’s when I realized that computer science (CS) could be applied in any area I wanted. I was hooked.

During my freshman year at BYU, I worked as a programmer for the Information Technology department. Later, I became a teaching assistant for three different CS classes, teaching weekly recitation sessions for CS 111 (Intro to CS), CS 236 (Discrete Structures), and CS 202 (Intro to Software Engineering).

Currently, I work in the Social Technology Privacy Lab under Dr. Xinru Page, where I’m developing a mobile app to help young adults with autism learn how to use social media. The app gamifies social media lessons into a self-paced course. Using CS to help people is incredibly fulfilling, and I plan to become a research professor. It’s the best path for me to do what I love—learning and creating tech that makes a difference.
